Islamic Terminology

Common terms you'll hear in Islamic conversations:

Bismillah (بِسْمِ اللهِ)

Meaning: "In the name of Allah" or "In the name of God"

Usage: Muslims say this before starting any action or activity to seek Allah's blessing.

Example: Before eating, reading, or starting work.

Inshallah (إِنْ شَاءَ اللهُ)

Meaning: "If Allah wills" or "God willing"

Usage: Used when talking about future plans or events, acknowledging that everything happens by Allah's will.

Example: "I'll see you tomorrow, inshallah."

Mashallah (مَا شَاءَ اللهُ)

Meaning: "What Allah has willed" or "As Allah has willed"

Usage: Expressed in praise, joy, or thankfulness for a blessing or achievement. Also used to ward off the evil eye.

Example: "Mashallah, your child has grown so much!"

Alhamdulillah (الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ)

Meaning: "All praise is due to Allah" or "Thanks be to God"

Usage: Expressing gratitude to Allah for His blessings and favors.

Example: "How are you?" "Alhamdulillah, I'm fine."

Subhanallah (سُبْحَانَ اللهِ)

Meaning: "Glory be to Allah" or "How perfect Allah is"

Usage: Expressing awe or amazement at Allah's creation or work.

Example: Upon seeing a beautiful sunset or witnessing something extraordinary.

Salah (Prayer) Guide

Guidance on performing the daily prayers:

Prayer Basics

The Five Daily Prayers:

  • Fajr: Dawn prayer (2 rakat)
  • Dhuhr: Noon prayer (4 rakat)
  • Asr: Afternoon prayer (4 rakat)
  • Maghrib: Sunset prayer (3 rakat)
  • Isha: Night prayer (4 rakat)

Prerequisites for Prayer:

  • Being in a state of purity (wudu or ghusl)
  • Clean body, clothes, and place of prayer
  • Covering the awrah (parts of the body that must be covered)
  • Facing the qiblah (direction of the Ka'bah)
  • Having the intention (niyyah) to pray

Prayer Positions & Actions

  1. Standing (Qiyam): Face the qiblah, raise hands to ears, and say "Allahu Akbar" (Takbir)
  2. Recitation (Qira'at): Recite Surah Al-Fatihah followed by any other Surah or verses
  3. Bowing (Ruku): Bend at waist, place hands on knees, say "Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem" three times
  4. Standing again (Qiyam): Rise saying "Sami' Allahu liman hamidah" then "Rabbana lakal hamd"
  5. Prostration (Sujood): Kneel and place forehead, nose, palms, knees, and toes on the ground, say "Subhana Rabbiyal A'la" three times
  6. Sitting between prostrations (Jalsah): Sit briefly saying "Allahu Akbar" then "Rabbi ghfirli"
  7. Second prostration: Same as the first
  8. Sitting for Tashahhud: After every 2 rakats, sit and recite the Tashahhud
  9. Final sitting (Tashahhud and Salawat): Recite Tashahhud and send blessings on Prophet Muhammad
  10. Tasleem: Turn head right saying "Assalamu alaikum wa rahmatullah," then left repeating the same

Joining Late or Missing Prayers

Joining a Prayer Late:

Basic rule: "Whatever you catch, pray it. And whatever you missed, complete it."

If you join during the first rakat:
  • Say "Allahu Akbar" and join the imam in whatever position he is in
  • You've caught that rakat if you joined before the imam rises from ruku (bowing)
  • Follow the imam until the end of prayer, then continue to complete what you missed
If you join in the middle of prayer:
  • The rakats you pray with the imam are considered your first rakats
  • After the imam finishes with tasleem, stand up and complete your remaining rakats
  • For example: If you join Dhuhr (4 rakats) during the 3rd rakat, pray 2 more rakats after the imam finishes

Making Up Missed Prayers:

If you miss a prayer entirely, make it up as soon as you remember or are able to. Pray it exactly as you would during its proper time.

Hajj and Umrah

Umrah: The Lesser Pilgrimage

Umrah can be performed at any time of the year and consists of four main rituals:

  1. Ihram: Enter the state of Ihram by wearing the special garments and making the intention for Umrah
  2. Tawaf: Circumambulate the Kaaba seven times in a counterclockwise direction
  3. Sa'i: Walk between the hills of Safa and Marwah seven times
  4. Halq or Taqsir: Cut or trim your hair to exit the state of Ihram

Hajj: The Major Pilgrimage

Hajj is performed during specific days of the Islamic month of Dhul-Hijjah. The basic steps include:

  1. 8th Dhul-Hijjah: Enter Ihram, then travel to Mina and stay there until Fajr
  2. 9th Dhul-Hijjah (Day of Arafah): Travel to Arafah after sunrise and stay until sunset, then travel to Muzdalifah for the night
  3. 10th Dhul-Hijjah: Collect pebbles at Muzdalifah, travel to Jamarat and stone the large Jamrah, perform animal sacrifice, cut or shave hair, perform Tawaf al-Ifadah and Sa'i
  4. 11th-13th Dhul-Hijjah: Stay in Mina and stone all three Jamarat each day
  5. Final step: Perform Tawaf al-Wida (Farewell Tawaf) before leaving Makkah

Preparations for Hajj and Umrah

  • Physical preparation: Build stamina for walking and standing long hours
  • Financial preparation: Ensure all expenses are covered with halal money and debts are paid
  • Spiritual preparation: Seek forgiveness, resolve conflicts, make sincere repentance
  • Educational preparation: Learn the rituals and duas for Hajj or Umrah
  • Practical preparation: Arrange travel documents, accommodation, and necessary items

Islamic Etiquette

Dealing with Neighbors

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) emphasized good treatment of neighbors:

  • Greet them with warmth and respect
  • Help them when they need assistance
  • Visit them when they are sick
  • Share food and gifts with them
  • Avoid disturbing them with noise or other nuisances
  • Respect their privacy
  • Be patient with any harm they may cause

Hadith: "Jibreel kept recommending treating neighbors with kindness until I thought he would assign a share of inheritance to them." (Bukhari and Muslim)

Managing Anger

The Prophet's teachings on controlling anger:

  • Seek refuge with Allah from Satan: "A'udhu billahi minash-shaytanir-rajim" (I seek refuge with Allah from Satan, the accursed)
  • Remain silent when angry
  • Change your position: if standing, sit down; if sitting, lie down
  • Perform wudu (ablution) to cool down
  • Remember that controlling anger is a sign of strength

Hadith: "The strong person is not the one who overcomes people, but the one who controls himself when angry." (Bukhari)

Social Interactions

  • Greeting: Say "Assalamu alaikum" (Peace be upon you) when meeting others
  • Smiling: Considered an act of charity in Islam
  • Visiting the sick: An important Islamic duty
  • Honoring guests: Treat guests with generosity and respect
  • Speaking kindly: Use gentle words and avoid harsh speech
  • Keeping promises: Fulfill commitments and promises
  • Respecting elders: Show special honor to older people
  • Being merciful to the young: Show kindness and compassion to children

Hadith: "He who does not show mercy to our young ones and respect to our old ones is not from among us." (Tirmidhi)

Hadith Teachings

Essential teachings from the sayings of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him):

Character and Morals

"The best of you are those who have the best character."

- Bukhari and Muslim

Islam places great emphasis on developing good character traits like honesty, kindness, generosity, and patience. Your character is a reflection of your faith.

"None of you truly believes until he wishes for his brother what he wishes for himself."

- Bukhari

True faith manifests in wanting good for others just as you want it for yourself. This promotes unity, empathy, and brotherhood in society.

Actions and Deeds

"Actions are judged by intentions, and everyone will get what they intended."

- Bukhari and Muslim

The value of your actions depends on your intentions. This hadith reminds us to purify our intentions and perform deeds sincerely for Allah's sake rather than for worldly gain or recognition.

"Whoever believes in Allah and the Last Day, let him speak good or remain silent."

- Bukhari and Muslim

A Muslim should be mindful of their speech. It's better to say something beneficial or remain silent than to engage in harmful, useless, or sinful talk.

Worship and Devotion

"Cleanliness is half of faith."

- Muslim

Physical and spiritual purity are essential in Islam. This includes bodily cleanliness, clean clothes, clean surroundings, and purity of heart and mind.

"The most beloved of deeds to Allah are those that are consistent, even if they are small."

- Bukhari and Muslim

Consistency in good deeds is more important than doing a lot occasionally. Regular small acts of worship and kindness are better than large but infrequent ones.
